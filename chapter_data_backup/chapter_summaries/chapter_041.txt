Chapter 41: In Mr. Tulkinghorn's Room

The murder investigation continues at Tulkinghorn's chambers. Mr. Bucket methodically examines every detail of the crime scene, looking for clues and evidence. Various people are questioned about their whereabouts and their relationships with the murdered lawyer. The investigation is thorough and systematic, with Bucket demonstrating his detective skills.

The chapter focuses on the physical investigation—examining the room where Tulkinghorn was killed, reconstructing the events of the murder, and trying to determine who had access and opportunity. Bucket looks at the entry points, the position of the body, the nature of the wound, and other forensic details. His method is careful and scientific, representing the new professional approach to criminal investigation.

Various characters come under scrutiny. People who had dealings with Tulkinghorn, people who had grievances against him, people who were seen in the area around the time of the murder—all are questioned and their stories examined. The net of investigation is widening, drawing in more and more people who had connections to the murdered lawyer.

The atmosphere is tense and claustrophobic. Tulkinghorn's chambers, which were his private domain where he kept so many secrets, are now being examined in minute detail. The investigation is not just about finding the murderer but also about uncovering the secrets Tulkinghorn held. As Bucket digs deeper, various hidden connections and relationships are coming to light. The murder investigation is becoming an investigation into the web of secrets and power that Tulkinghorn had created around himself.
